What is the Pomodoro Technique?


The Pomodoro Technique is a time management strategy developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. At its core, this method involves using a timer to work in focused bursts of 25 minutes, known as "pomodori." The technique's name is derived from the Italian word for tomato ("pomodoro"), a nod to the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo used during his university days.


This structured approach breaks the work process into intervals separated by short breaks. It draws inspiration from iterative cycles and agile practices in software development, including pair programming. The central premise is that frequent breaks can enhance mental agility. Research has indicated that these periodic rest intervals can positively influence memory retention.


The Pomodoro Technique can be summarized in five steps:


  • Choose a specific task to work on
  • Set the timer (pomodoro) for a 25-minute work session
  • Work on the task uninterrupted until the timer goes off, then mark that session as complete
  • Take a brief 5-10 minute break
  • After completing four pomodori, indulge in a longer break ranging from 20-25 minutes.
